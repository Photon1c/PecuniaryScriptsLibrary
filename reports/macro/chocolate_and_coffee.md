### Top Raw Cacao (Cocoa Beans) Exporting Countries

| Rank | Country              | Notes                                                                 |
|------|----------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1    | Côte d'Ivoire (Ivory Coast) | World's largest producer and exporter; supplies ~40–45% of global cacao. |
| 2    | Ghana                | Second-largest exporter; provides ~15–20% of global supply.           |
| 3    | Ecuador              | Major Latin American exporter; known for fine/flavor cacao; often ranks 3rd or 4th. |
| 4    | Cameroon             | Significant West African exporter; consistently in the top five.       |
| 5    | Nigeria              | Historically large producer; remains a key West African exporter despite fluctuations. |
| 6    | Indonesia            | Leading Asian producer; exports substantial raw beans despite domestic processing. |
| 7    | Peru                 | Growing exporter of high-quality, often organic, fine flavor cacao.   |
| 8    | Dominican Republic   | Smaller volume but respected for high-quality, organic, and fair-trade cacao. |
| 9    | Uganda               | Emerging African producer; steadily increasing exports.               |
| 10   | Colombia             | Focuses on specialty and fine flavor cacao; export volumes growing.   |

> **Key Trends:**  
> - **West Africa Dominance:** Côte d'Ivoire and Ghana together account for over 60% of global raw cacao exports.  
> - **Fine Flavor Cacao:** Ecuador, Peru, and the Dominican Republic are vital to the specialty chocolate market due to unique flavor profiles, despite lower volumes.  
> - **Sustainability and Certification:** There is a growing global emphasis on certified sustainable, ethical, and traceable cacao across all major exporting regions.

### Top Coffee Exporting Countries (Green and Roasted Beans)

| Rank | Country     | Notes                                                                 |
|------|-------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1    | Brazil      | World's largest coffee producer and exporter (mainly Arabica).        |
| 2    | Vietnam     | Second-largest exporter; dominates the Robusta market.                |
| 3    | Colombia    | Famous for high-quality Arabica; consistently a top exporter.         |
| 4    | Indonesia   | Major exporter of both Arabica and Robusta, including specialty coffees. |
| 5    | Ethiopia    | Birthplace of Arabica coffee; significant exporter with unique flavors. |
| 6    | Honduras    | Largest coffee producer in Central America; substantial exporter.     |
| 7    | Uganda      | Leading African exporter, primarily of Robusta coffee.                |
| 8    | India       | Exports both Arabica and Robusta, often shade-grown.                  |
| 9    | Peru        | Known for organic and fair-trade Arabica; growing export volumes.     |
| 10   | Guatemala   | Produces high-quality, regionally diverse Arabica; key exporter.      |

> **Note:** Trade data can fluctuate year by year due to harvest conditions, global demand, and economic factors. These lists represent the general top players in recent times (2022–2023).
